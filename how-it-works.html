Our Process

From your measurements
to your door

Tilf Habesha is not a fashion retailer. It is a digital ordering system for traditional Ethiopian tailoring — connecting you directly with the hands that make your dress.

Step by Step

Five steps from browse to doorstep

1
Step One

Browse & Choose Your Dress

Explore our curated collection of Habesha styles from certified artisans across Ethiopia. Each listing shows the artisan's name, city, rating, delivery time, and price — so you know exactly who is making your dress before you order.

Use category filters to find Wedding Kemis, Holiday dresses, Netela, everyday wear, or men's traditional Habesha. Every product tells you the fabric, tibeb style, and minimum stitching days.

💡 Tip: Save your favourites with the ♡ button. You can return to them anytime from your Wishlist — even across devices when you're signed in.
2
Step Two

Enter Your Exact Measurements

Unlike standard sizing, every Tilf Habesha dress is cut specifically for your body. On the product page you'll enter five measurements in centimeters. Use a soft measuring tape and have someone help you for accuracy.

3
Step Three

Pay Your 15% Deposit

We don't ask you to pay the full amount upfront. A 15% deposit secures your order and signals to the artisan that they can begin cutting and stitching your fabric. This model protects you and compensates the artisan fairly for starting work.

Only 15% to start.

On a $300 dress, you pay just $45 today. The remaining $255 is only charged when your dress is complete, quality-checked, and ready to ship.

💡 Note: If for any reason the artisan cannot fulfill your order, your deposit is fully refunded — no questions asked.

5
Step Five

Pay the Balance & Receive Your Dress

When your dress passes quality check and is ready to leave the workshop, we notify you to pay the remaining 85%. Once confirmed, your dress is carefully packaged and shipped worldwide via DHL, FedEx, or EMS — your choice.

Shipping typically takes 5–10 business days internationally. You'll receive tracking information as soon as your parcel is dispatched from Addis Ababa.

💡 Diaspora tip: Many customers in the US, UK, and Europe order 6–8 weeks before a special occasion to be comfortably on time. Artisan stitching time ranges from 14–35 days depending on complexity.
Questions

Frequently asked questions

What if my dress doesn't fit? +
Because your dress is made to your exact measurements, fit issues are extremely rare. However, if there is a significant deviation from your submitted measurements, we work with the artisan to alter the dress at no extra cost. Please measure carefully and include any special notes on the order form.
How long does the whole process take? +
Total time from order to delivery is typically 3–6 weeks. Stitching takes 14–35 days depending on the dress complexity and artisan workload. International shipping adds 5–10 business days. We recommend ordering at least 6 weeks before your event date.
Is my deposit refundable? +
If an artisan cannot fulfill your order, your deposit is fully refunded within 5–7 business days. Once stitching has begun (usually within 48–72 hours of payment), deposits are non-refundable as the artisan has already sourced fabric and started work. Orders that are complete but where the 85% balance goes unpaid for more than 14 days will be forfeited.
Can I customise the design — colours, sleeve length, etc.? +
Yes! The Notes to Artisan field on the order form is your space for customisation requests: specific tibeb colours, sleeve length (short, 3/4, full), collar style, or any other preferences. The artisan will confirm availability before starting. Some deep customisations may incur a small additional charge, which the artisan will communicate before proceeding.
Do you ship worldwide? +
Yes. We ship to all countries via DHL Express, FedEx, or EMS. Shipping costs are calculated at checkout based on your country and package weight. Most diaspora customers are in the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Germany, Sweden, and Australia — all well-served routes from Addis Ababa.
How do I become a supplier / artisan? +
Skilled tailors and workshops in Ethiopia can apply through the Become a Supplier section on our homepage. We review applications, conduct a quality verification, and onboard accepted artisans with a dedicated profile page. There are no upfront fees — we take a small platform commission only when you earn. Support is available in Amharic and English.
